Coliseum

Talk motorsport, together with the best racing personalities.

Is motorsport dying? That's what our client team was feeling when they first came to MetaLab. The focus for this project was to help Coliseum design a vision for how to better engage motorsport fans and grow the sport organically within the USA. The business opportunity lay in tapping into and growing the younger generations of motorsport fans by renewing their connection to the sport through their own playing field – social media.

These types of projects are usually the most fun as I really get to push my imagination and am not confined by the usual limitations of actaully building a product. This time around was no different!

Talk Motorsport, 24-7

As with most projects like this, we started with a round of tarantino's. Which is basically where we go wild in imaging a vision for the product. We aligned on a strategy to build a social audio platform that focuses on live streaming conversations before, during and after races. The idea was that the conversation would always be going, as opposed to using mediums like video where that might not be the case.

Within a a few weeks, of moderated user interviews and a thorough competitor audit, we landed on some solid insights that helped has propel forward into the design phase:

  • Clear barriers to entry still exist within motorsports.
  • Motorsport content does not adapt to fans' learning styles or knowledge levels.
  • The current fan experience facilitates passive spectatorship as opposed to active participation.
  • Building psychological safety in digital spaces is a challenge, particularly as communities scale.

    • Levelling the playing field.

    One of the issues we picked up in our research was that some people love to just listen in, and don't have the desire to contribute in the same way others do. Also, much of the time discussions are dominated by certain individuals, and that affects participation from others. So an important product and user problem became about finding ways to empower listeners to participate in the conversation in ways that made them comfortable and creating a more democratic listening environment. This had both user and business value by means of increasing retention rate.

    To make live audio rooms more fair and to encourage equal participation, we designed a feature that allows you to submit and vote on topics that matter to you, which are then discussed alongside your favorite motorsport heroes. We also designed a feature that allows you to preview streams and topics before diving in, empowering people to understand context before comitting to diving in completely. Additionally, we developed a set of basic reactions that allow people to emotionally connect without speaking up in the channel.

    The finish line

    Our solution invloved a unique streaming experience that set Coliseum apart from its competitors. It's immersive, and interactive, and allows people to engage with their motorsport heoroes, such as Max Verstappen and Louis Hamilton, as well as other fans in a way that makes them feel like they're not just another voice in the crowd.

    Working closely with Eilís (our researcher) to develop and test concepts was a dream. I also got a chance to grow my time and stakeholder management skills having had to develop the designs and running our concepting phase. I had a blast collaborating with Nuno (our Principal Designer) and Cam (Design lead) to bring our strategic direction to life and designing out and prototyping solutions. To the best of my knowledge, the designs have yet to be built, but I'm eager to see if this takes off in the future!
