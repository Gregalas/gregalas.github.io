COLISEUM

Talk motorsport, together with the best racing personalities.

THE BRIEF

This project was about helping an early-stage startup called Coliseum design a vision for how to better engage motorsport fans and grow the sport organically within the USA. Our client saw a business opportunity in tapping into and growing the younger generations of motorsport fans by renewing their connection to the sport and other fans using social media.

These types of projects are often the most fun, as I really get to push my imagination and am not confined by the usual limitations of an end-to-end build. Don’t get me wrong, I love working end-to-end and using technical constraints to to help mould an outcome, but at times it's great to have complete creative freedom, too!

A 2 MONTH RACE TO DEVELOP A CONCEPT MVP

As with most projects like this, we started with an idea of what the product could be and used competitor research to begin validating our direction and drawing on inspiration from products like HiHo, Twitter Spaces, Clubhouse, Spotify Live and even Twitch. I ran the competitor audit and worked with our researcher to interview motorsports fans that fit our “gamer motorsport fan” persona – we saw this cohort as the most likely to adopt the product. Within a few weeks we landed on some really solid insights and opportunities which informed our design concepts.

  • Clear barriers to entry still exist within motorsports.
  • Motorsport content does not adapt to fans' learning styles or knowledge levels.
  • The current fan experience facilitates passive spectatorship as opposed to active participation.
  • Building psychological safety in digital spaces is a challenge, particularly as communities scale.

    • THE SOLVE: EMPOWERING FANS and creating equality

    One of the issues we picked up in our research was that some people love to just listen in, and don't have the desire to contribute in the same way others do. We weren't 100% clear on why, but had the theory that a lot of the time discussions are dominated by certain individuals, and that affects participation from others significantly. We also found that transparency around disucssions isn't always clear. So an important product and user problem became about finding ways to empower listeners to participate in the conversation in ways that made them comfortable and creating a more democratic environment. This had both user and business value by means of increasing retention rate.

    To make live audio rooms more conversational and fair, we gave fans the capacity to submit and vote on topics that matter to them, which are then discussed alongside their favorite motorsport heroes. We also designed the ability to proview streams and topics before diving in, empowering people to understand context before comitting to diving in completely. Additionally, we developed a set of basic reactions that allow people to emotionally connect without speaking up in the channel.

    THE RESULT

    Our solution invloved a unique streaming experience that set Coliseum apart from its competitors. It's immersive, and interactive, and allows people to engage with their motorsport heoroes, such as Max Verstappen and Louis Hamilton, as well as other fans in a way that makes them feel like they're not just another voice in the crowd.

    Working closely with Eilís (our researcher) to develop and test concepts was a dream. I also got a chance to grow my time and stakeholder management skills having had to develop the designs and running our concepting phase. I had a blast collaborating with Nuno (our Principal Designer) and Cam (Design lead) to bring our strategic direction to life and designing out and prototyping solutions. To the best of my knowledge, the designs have yet to be built, but I'm eager to see if this takes off in the future!
