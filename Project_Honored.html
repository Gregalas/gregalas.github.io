HonorEd

Reimagining online education

Imagine a world where all people have access to an advanced education of the highest standards no matter where they are in world... An education that allows the flexiblity to work from anywhere, while still fostering creative and critical thinking and a sense of community.

Over a 6 month period our team at MetaLab helped Honor Eduction envision that world. I jumped in at a critical stage of the project to work on key features that would help realise their mission of democratizing university level education.

My focus

I came into the project with 3 months remaining to help rapidly solve problems within 3 key areas of the product.

  1. Time management: Help students keep pace with the course material
  2. Student and teacher engagement: Help students build and maintain connection with their peers and teachers as much as possible.
  3. Interacting with live and async content: Ensure that students can engage with all content mediums in a fun and interactive way.

Empowering students through better time management

We designed a flexible class and module planner to help students manage their time and lives on their own terms. The planner encourages students to take control of their schedules and learn time management skills. It allows them to shift classes, provides session recordings, and suggests recommended study times.
To make the planner even more flexible, we designed two views: "Course view" allows you to organize your planner based on the courses you're taking. "Type view" enables you to see your planner organized by module or assigments.

An activity feed that keeps students connected

The feed is designed to work in unison with the planner. It functions as a conventional To-do list by highlighting specific items within the timeline that are coming up or are due next. The intent is to nudge positive behaviour while avoiding a sense of “being told what to do”. It helps create a connection between your actions and workload, and how you can best manage it.

Bulding confidence through research

We had a great time developing tools for student engagement and feedback, using emojis as a fun way to gather student's reactions and measure outcomes. To avoid jumping into research without a solid plan, we used client workshops to help refine our approach first.

Because emojis are so subjective, my team mate and I designed fun client workshop where we picked an emoji that best described a reaction, and then compared results. Using a more refined emoji set, we tested 4 reactions with actual students by showing them a video or have them read article and then document their reactions. In the end we were far more confident in what each emoji represented to the majority of students.
To react to content using a consistent cross platform mechanism, we landed on a contextual action menu that does not obstruct content and is consistent across all platforms. With this menu you can quickly flag a moment or item as Important, Confusing or Custom, later updated to the - Clarifying, Exciting, Puzzling and Thought-Provoking.
