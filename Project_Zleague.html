Z League

The world's first skill-based tournament platform for casual gamers.

Why do amateur gamers get a raw deal when it comes to competing online?

This is what my team and I at MetaLab set out to solve with our client, Z League, a tournament gaming platform aiming to make esports fair and fun for amateurs.

The big problem was that amateur gamers can easily watch streamers and pros compete in tournaments, but there were no good options to compete themselves, atleast for higher stakes, fairly. The ask from Z League was to take their proprietary tech – which eliminates bias in matches – and re-imagine their tournament platform on mobile.

How it all started

We began the 6-month project by designing the vision MVP for the product and developing an interim brand while Koto worked on the final version.

Enhancing tournament discovery: The adaptive tournament feed

A key part of the experience was tournament discovery and the buildup, so we made sure to design the "find a tournament" entry point to be easily accessible and the tournament schedule to be dynamic and adaptive. Upcoming and live tournaments are displayed in a "dark zone" that creates excitement and builds anticipation.

We also designed a super smooth and efficient tournament registration flow that helps you build a team and register in just a few seconds; one of Z Leagues most important KPI’s

Leveraging research: Diving deep into the minds of amateurs

Not being an avid gamer meant I needed to pay extra attention to research, and constantly be learning about the world of gaming – one of my favorite aspects of being a product designer! Aside from strengthening my "gamer street slang", research provided several key insights that influenced feature decisions both for the MVP and future roadmap.

For example, we learned that for amateurs it’s not all about the cash, but competing in tournaments with their closest friends is one of the key reason they use the platform! So the question we ended up solving for was about how we might create a registration and teams experience that prioritises your close circle of friends over playing with random folk who you may not vibe with, while still making things competitive and fun.

The result

Within 6 months, we redesigned the Z League app for mobile and released it to an eager user base. It's a great feeling knowing that thanks to our team, hundreds of thousands of gamers around the world are able to compete for cash fairly while having fun with their friends.

Importantly, we created business impact by helping shape Z League's future roadmap with exciting features that will ensure they’ll remain competitive within their category, and continue providing value and enjoyment to their users.
