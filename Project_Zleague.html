Z League

The world's first skill-based tournament platform for casual gamers.

Why do amateur gamers get a raw deal when it comes to competing online? The big problem was that amateur gamers could easily watch streamers and pros compete, but there were no good options to compete themselves, at least fairly and for higher stakes.

In steps MetaLab! Our goal was to help redefine the Z League product in a way that improves the overall experience for players, and increases tournament registration and retention so players keep coming back for more. Within 4 months we designed and released their first ever mobile application.

I was involved at all stages of the design process, including:

  • Defining the product and establishing Z League's Vision MVP.
  • Product strategy, prioritisation and roadmapping.
  • User experience and hi-fidelity design.
  • Designs systems.

    • Key moments

    There was a ton of work that went into defining and designing the vision and initial feature set for the first release of the product, but for now here are some of the design challenges we solved that are worth mentioning:

    Enhancing tournament discovery: The adaptive tournament feed

    A key part of the experience was tournament discovery and buildup, so we made sure to design the "find a tournament" entry point to be easily accessible and the tournament schedule to be dynamic and adaptive. Upcoming and live tournaments are displayed in a "dark zone" that creates excitement and builds anticipation.

    We also designed a super smooth and efficient tournament registration flow that helps you build a team and register in just a few seconds.

    Leveraging research: Diving deep into the minds of amateurs

    Not being an avid gamer meant I needed to pay extra attention to research, and constantly be learning about the world of gaming – one of my favorite aspects of being a product designer! Aside from strengthening my "gamer street slang", research provided several key insights that influenced feature decisions both for the MVP and future roadmap.

    For example, we learned that for amateurs it’s not all about the cash, but competing in tournaments with their closest friends is one of the key reason they use the platform! So the question we ended up solving for was about how we might create a registration and teams experience that prioritises your close circle of friends over playing with random folk who you may not vibe with, while still making things competitive and fun.

    The result

    Within 6 months, we redesigned the Z League app for mobile and released it to an eager user base.

    Importantly, we created business impact by helping shape Z League's future roadmap with exciting features that will ensure they’ll remain competitive within their category, and continue providing value and enjoyment to their users.
