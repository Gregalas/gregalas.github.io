Z LEAGUE

The world's first skill-based tournament platform casual gamers.

THE BRIEF

Z League exists because some amateur gamers like watching streamers and pros compete in tournaments, but there were no good options to compete themselves.

The ask from Z League was to take their proprietary technology – which uses of a powerful algorithm that eliminates bias in matches and places people into skill based divisions – and re-imagine their product on mobile in a way that would empower amateur gamers to compete for cash and level the playing field in esports.

WHAT WE DID

The experience was centered around tournament discovery and buildup. Because of the business and user needs around the speed at which people re-enter tournaments, we never wanted gamers to lose sight of the “find a tournament” entry point. The tournament schedule was designed to be dynamic and adaptive – depending on how close your tournament is to being live would determine its position and design treatment. We used a “dark zone” for upcoming and live tournament cards, that inherit an immersive and darker feel to create excitement and exaggerate the buildup. 

RESEARCH DRIVEN

Not being an avid gamer motivated me to be even more in tune with our research outputs, and to be learning about the idiosyncrasies of our unique audience. Aside from strengthening my "gamer street slang" , we picked up several key insights that influenced feature decisions both acutely for immediate product and design decisions, and in terms of macro-prioritisation.

For example, it was important we provided options for you to jump into a game at just about any time while considering the insight that amateur gamers prefer to play with their close circle of friends. For them, it wasn't only about the cash, but the camaraderie around teams, too! As a result, we designed the registration experience and teams feature to prioritise your close circle of friends over playing with random folk with whom you may not 'click'.

THE RESULT

Within seven months my team and I designed and built the Z League MVP and released it to market while running a parallel vision track for design to establish a solid runway for the Z League team to help elevate their product with features such as a customizable profile and teams – many of these features are in their app today.

I worked end-to-end executing at all stages of the product design process all the way from influencing the strategic direction of the project to the experience and visual design working hand-in-hand with our developers to ensure we had spec-ready designs for build.
