Z League

The world's first skill-based tournament platform casual gamers.

Why do amateur gamers get a raw deal when it comes to competing online? This is what my project team and I at MetaLab set out to solve with our client, Z League. The big problem was that amateur gamers can easily watch streamers and pros compete in tournaments, but there were no good options to compete themselves.

The ask from Z League was to take their proprietary technology – which uses of a powerful algorithm that eliminates bias in matches and places people into skill based divisions – and re-imagine their product on mobile in a way that would empower amateur gamers to compete for cash and level the playing field in esports. "Why mobile?" you ask... "Because people love to monitor, track, and sign up for live tournaments no matter where they are or what they're doing.

Let the games begin...

We began the 6-month project by designing the vision MVP for the product and developing an interim brand while Koto worked on the final version.

A key part of the experience was tournament discovery and buildup, so we made sure to keep the "find a tournament" entry point easily accessible to users and designed the tournament schedule to be dynamic and adaptive, with upcoming and live tournaments showcased in a "dark zone" that creates excitement and builds anticipation.

Research driven

Not being an avid gamer motivated me to be even more in tune with our research outputs, and to be learning about the idiosyncrasies of our unique audience. Aside from strengthening my "gamer street slang" , we picked up several key insights that influenced feature decisions both acutely for immediate product and design decisions, and in terms of macro-prioritisation.

For example, it was important we provided options for you to jump into a game at just about any time while considering the insight that amateur gamers prefer to play with their close circle of friends. For them, it wasn't only about the cash, but the camaraderie around teams, too! As a result, we designed the registration experience and teams feature to prioritise your close circle of friends over playing with random folk with whom you may not 'click'.

The result

Within 6 months my team and I designed and built the Z League MVP and released it to market. What's more, using the vision MVP work we did, we helped Z LEAGUE establish a solid roadmap to elevate their product with features such as customizable player and team profiles, that I'm so stoked to see in the app today!

Overall, I really enjoyed how involved I was in this project, as I was able to contribute to the strategic direction and design process from start to finish. From developing an interim visual language to designing and building out an exceptional tournament experience alongside our engineers, I had a ton of fun and built some great friendships along the way. Despite the various technical and legal limitations we had to work with, we were able to overcome them and create something truly remarkable.
