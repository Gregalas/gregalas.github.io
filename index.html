First, philosophy

These principles provide a snapshot of how I like to think about leadership, product, and design and lean on these whenever I can when making decicions.
  1. Keep it simple. Being overly clever or innovating unnecessarily will hide your ideas. When you’re forced to be simple you’re forced to face the real problem.
  2. Build products that are real. Make things that people can actually use and enjoy. By solving problems that bring meaning to people's lives, you'll in turn do the same for yourself.
  3. Innovate responsibly. To innovate also means creating new rules around responsibility. Don't be naive to the power software has to influence people. “With great power comes great responsibility”. - Uncle Ben
  4. Promote objectivity and find the truth. Focus on getting things right, as opposed to being right.
  5. Pay it forward. People internanlise knowledge and learn the most through actions. Teach skills by giving people a chance.

Then craft

I love to create simple solutions to problems other people seem to overlook and that really need to be solved. My approach involves delivering solutions informally, beginning with the simplest version and then iterating rapidly.
