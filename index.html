First, philosophy

A couple of north star principles that are foundational to how I think about design, teams and building magical products.
  1. Force simplicity. Being overly clever or innovating unnecessarily hides your ideas. Forcing simplicity helps you confront the real problem.
  2. Build products that are real. Make things that people actually find useful and enjoy. Every detail counts.
  3. Know where your dopamine is coming from. The answer determines your behaviour. Rather get your dopamine from improving things and innovating, as opposed to being validated.
  4. We know far less than we think. Many of the things we believe are wrong. Your job is to find the truth, not to be right.
  5. Creative energy compounds. If you get the right peope in the room together your ideas will fly!
  6. Create exponential feedback. Most feedback is surface level. Find the root of the problem and teach people frameworks to help themselves.
  7. Share early. Share often. Keeping quiet usually just limits progress.

Then craft

I love to create simple solutions to problems other people seem to overlook and that really need to be solved. My approach involves delivering solutions informally, beginning with the simplest version and then iterating rapidly.
