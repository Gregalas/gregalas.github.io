Hey, I'm Greg

I'm a Senior Product Designer at MetaLab, where I've been helping build products for early stage startups from proof of concept to launch, and evolving products for large enterprise level organisations.

Previously I was a Lead Product Designer at a startup called 22seven and have worked at several other award-winning digital design agencies.

First, philosophy

I have always gravitated toward using principles to help frame my thinking and guide my decisions in everyday life, and at work as a design leader.
  1. Force simplicity. Being overly clever or innovating unnecessarily hides your ideas. Forcing simplicity helps you confront the real problem.
  2. Build products that are real. Make things that people actually find useful and enjoy. This is how you find fullfillment.
  3. Innovate responsibly. To innovate also means creating new rules around responsibility. Don't be naive to the power software has to influence people.
  4. Know where your dopamine is coming from. The answer determines your behaviour. Rather get your dopamine from improving things and innovating, as opposed to being validated.
  5. We know far less than we think. Many of the things we believe are wrong. Your job is to find the truth, not to be right. We are often not even asking the right questions.
  6. You're capable of more than you think. Your only limitation is yourself, and the laws of physics. You're here to fulfill your potential. Your potential is infinite.
  7. Create exponential feedback. Most feedback is surface level. Find the root of the problem and teach people frameworks to help themselves.
  8. Driven by impact. Things tend to fall in place when you're continuously asking how you can create more value.

Then craft

I love to create simple solutions to problems other people seem to overlook and that really need to be solved. My approach involves delivering solutions informally, beginning with the simplest version and then iterating rapidly.
