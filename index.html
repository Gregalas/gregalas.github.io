First, philosophy

Here are a couple of north star principles that I believe are foundational to building the best products in the world. These as starting points, not conclusions.
  1. Build products that are real. Make things that people find useful and enjoy.
  2. Optimize for magic and surprise. People don’t talk about a product doing exactly what they expected it to do. They talk about a product doing what they didn’t expect.
  3. Know where your dopamine is coming from. The answer determines your behaviour. Rather get your dopamine from improving things and innovating, as opposed to being validated.
  4. Creative energy compounds. People are more important than ideas. If you get the right people in the room together your ideas will fly!
  5. Create exponential feedback. Most feedback is surface level. Find the root of the problem and teach people frameworks to help themselves.
  6. Share early. Share often. Keeping quiet usually just limits progress. Keep communication lines open.

Then craft

I love to create simple solutions to problems other people seem to overlook and that really need to be solved. My approach involves delivering solutions informally, beginning with the simplest version and then iterating rapidly.
