Acapela

Better hybrid collaboration, fewer meetings, more time for you.

Do meetings suck, or do we just suck at running them? It‚Äôs a critical question that dives deep into the social dynamics and status quo of how we've been operating in the modern workplace.

My squad and I at MetaLab teamed up with Acapela, a startup who's mission it was to end meetings before they start, or at least make meetings more effective and help people focus on their most important work.

The problem with meetings today

After a couple of weeks of research and concept design, we identified three main themes that caught our attention:

  • Accessibility and overwhelm: Teams struggle with poor access to information and tool fatigue.
  • Loss of control: Folks can‚Äôt protect their time because they don‚Äôt feel in control, yet they experience "meeting FOMO".
  • The status quo of meetings: Async is perceived as a threat to morale, quality, and productivity.
    • These insights got us thinking about a new version of Acapela with fresh take on the value proposition. We wanted to explore communication methods that could really amp up team dynamics and boost productivity.

    The solve

    We envisioned a world where meetings transformed into collaborative workspaces, enabling teams to resolve their meetings before they even start. However, we faced the challenge of striking a balance between protecting people's time and ensuring that these workspaces truly enhanced work efficiency. It was crucial for these spaces to foster connection and boost morale, despite their asynchronous-first approach.

    Protecting people‚Äôs time, with topics

    In order to maximize productivity and efficiency, we introduced the concept of a 'topic' to facilitate pre-meeting discussions. A topic represents an agenda item that requires action, either before the meeting begins or synchronously if it remains unresolved. By ensuring sufficient resolution of topics, we automatically remove the corresponding meeting from your calendar.

    We coined the term "rooms" to describe these collaborative workspaces, resembling Slack in some ways. However, rooms are more action-oriented, aiming to eradicate aimless conversations and time wastage. An excellent example of their functionality is the ability to effortlessly convert comments into actionable next steps or topics, enhancing discoverability through tagging.

    Fostering connection and prioritizing time

    By default, spaces and rooms are designed to be open and accessible. If a meeting is scheduled on the calendar, you're encouraged to explore the AI-generated meeting notes, quickly review the key discussion points, and even contribute if you find it necessary.
